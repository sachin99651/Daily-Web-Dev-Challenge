Alan Turing

The man who pioneered modern computing and artificial intelligence

Timeline of a Visionary:

  • 1912 - Born in Maida Vale, London.
  • 1936 - Published "On Computable Numbers," introducing the concept of the "Turing Machine."
  • 1939 - Joined Bletchley Park to help crack German naval codes during WWII.
  • 1945 - Devised the first design for a stored-program computer.
  • 1950 - Proposed the "Turing Test" to determine if a machine can demonstrate human-like intelligence.
  • 1954 - Passed away, leaving a legacy that shapes our digital world today.

"Sometimes it is the people no one can imagine anything of who do the things no one can imagine."
